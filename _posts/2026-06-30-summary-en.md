## [EML Trees Proven to be Universal Approximators](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1uipl1t/eml_trees_are_universal_approximators_r/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A theoretical proof demonstrates that EML(-type) trees are universal approximators, capable of representing any elementary function through composition. This breakthrough extends the capabilities of EML trees to approximate complex functional spaces, including polynomials and continuous functions within Sobolev spaces. This theoretical result provides a strong mathematical foundation for EML trees, suggesting their potential for modeling a wide range of complex functions in machine learning. It implies that EML trees, with bounded size and depth, could be used to approximate arbitrary functions in various functional spaces. The proof involves constructing explicit representations for binary operations, polynomials, and hyperbolic tangent using EML(-type) functions as building blocks. Technical challenges, such as handling the non-positive domain of the natural logarithm, were addressed through sign-based decompositions and affine maps.

**Background**: Universal approximation theorems in machine learning state that certain neural network structures can approximate any continuous function to a desired accuracy. EML trees are a computational primitive that can be mapped to hardware or used for symbolic regression. Sobolev spaces are vector spaces of functions equipped with norms that measure the function and its derivatives, important for solving partial differential equations.
